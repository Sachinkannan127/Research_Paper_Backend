Abstract
In this positioning paper, we evaluate the problem-solving and reason-
ing capabilities of contemporary Large Language Models (LLMs) through
their performance inZork, the seminal text-based adventure game first
released in 1977. The game’s dialogue-based structure provides a con-
trolled environment for assessing how LLM-based chatbots interpret nat-
ural language descriptions and generate appropriate action sequences to
succeed in the game. We test the performance of leading proprietary
models—ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini—under both minimal and de-
tailed instructions, measuring game progress through achieved scores as
the primary metric. Our results reveal that all tested models achieve less
than 10% completion on average, with even the best-performing model

2.2 Metacognition in LLMs
While metacognition has been extensively studied in human psychology [10,
29], relatively little work addresses metacognitive capabilities in AI systems.
Some researchers have explored confidence calibration in neural networks [11]
and uncertainty estimation [17], but these approaches differ fundamentally from
human metacognition’s adaptive, reflective qualities.
Mitchell [23, 24] argues that current AI systems lack the self-monitoring
necessary to recognize when they are “stuck” and need to change strategies—a

6 Limitations
This positioning paper has several limitations that should be acknowledged. Our
experimental design includes five runs per setting, which limits statistical power
and prevents definitive claims about the absence of differences between settings.
Moreover, we lack human player baselines, making it difficult to contextualize
whether an average∼10% completion represents failure or reasonable perfor-
mance on a genuinely difficult game. We cannot rule out training data contam-
ination, as models may have encountered Zork walkthroughs during training,
although the established performance suggests little to no contamination. Our
qualitative analysis draws on illustrative examples rather than systematic analy-
